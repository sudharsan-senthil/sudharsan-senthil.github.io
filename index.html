Projects

Recovering the Influence Graph of a High-Dimensional Markov Process.

A high-dimensional Markov process can be used to model a social network with or without memory. We derived the sample complexity for a recursive greedy algorithm to fully recover the underlying influence graph, when the Markov chain (the whole network) probabilistically moves back and forth. Such assumption on the chain was introduced to encompass the behavioral patterns of infrequent social media users. This work was my BTech thesis, adviced by Prof. Avhishek Chatterjee. code, and presentation.

Data-Driven Learning of Channel Codes

Siamese is a novel framework to jointly learn the encodings of two interfering users while mutually exclusively learning the decodings. Empirical results show that the network outperforms TDMA for a wide range of interference strenghts while being resilient to changing channel conditions. Some new cost functions to incentivize the encoder to learn the capacity achieving codes are also derived for the single user scenario. code.

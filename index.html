Projects

Recovering the Influence Graph of a High-Dimensional Markov Process.

A simple social network can be modeled using a high-dimensional Markov process with or without memory. We derived the sample complexity for a recursive greedy algorithm to fully recover the underlying influence graph, when the Markov chain(or whole network) probabilistically moves back and forth. Such assumption on the chain was introduced to encompass the behavioral patters of infrequent social media users. This work was my BTech thesis project, adviced by Prof. Avhishek Chatterjee.

Data-Driven Learning of Channel Codes

A novel training framework (Siamese) to jointly learn the encoders of two interfering users while mutually exclusively learning the decoders, is developed analysed. Empirical results show that the network outperforms TDMA for a wide range of interference strenghts while being resilient to changing channel conditions. Some new cost functions to incentivize learning the capacity achieving codes are also experimented in the single user scenario.
